The Three Revolutions that changed History!

Discover more about the impacts, effects, and benefits of the three revolutions below!

Agricultural Revolution

In the Agricultural Revolution, there was growth in settled societies, domestication of plants and animals, and a transition from nomadic living to permanent settlement.


It initiated a serious turn of events in human history toward organized farming, extra food production, and laying the base for complex social structures.

Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution means the age of creating mechanical methods of production based on wide-scale use of steam power, later electricity, enormous advances in infrastructure for transportation and communication, and explosive growth of urban centers as industrial locations.


It changed the way economies, societies, and everyday life were organized, setting the world onto an unprecedented course of technological innovation and productivity growth.

Industrial Revolution

Digital Revolution

The development of networked computing systems, proliferation of both personal and mobile computers, internet and World Wide Web, digital communication platforms, and exponential growth in digital data attended the Digital Revolution.


It changed the way information was created, accessed, shared, and stored and thus created profound revolutions across industries, economies, and global connectivity.
